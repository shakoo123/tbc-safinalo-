The origin and further development of the city was determined by the industry formed on the basis of the manganese ore deposit in the Kvirili river valley. The foundation of the city was laid in 1879, when the mining of manganese ore began on the initiative of Akaki Tsereteli. Chiaturi Black Stone Mining Society was founded, which was engaged in manganese mining. In 1895, a railway was built that connected Chiaturi railway station to Shorapani. During this period, about 6 thousand people worked in Chiatura, and the share of manganese in the world export reached 50%. Built in the 19th century, only the right side of the Kvirili river was built, old Chiatura was known for its narrow streets, which arose spontaneously, without planning.

In this part, the historical development and the network of streets have been preserved. In 1892, the English built the first tennis court in Chiatura, Georgia. The development of tennis in Georgia dates back to the 19th century.In Chiatura, the manager of the English firm "Forward and Salinas", John Tarsay, introduced a new game, lawn tennis, to Manganum city. The prominent English tennis player and teacher Ian Homer, who worked in Chiatura, mentions in his memoirs that the Tsereteli family and Akaki Tsereteli watched the tennis spectacle. From Chiaturi, tennis spread to Tbilisi and Batumi. Chiatura was declared a city in 1921.Since 1921, Chiathura has been celebrating the town's holiday "Chiaturuba", which is attended by fa mous Georgians and representatives of towns friendly with Chiathura.

In 1924, an uprising against the Soviet regime began in almost all major cities of Georgia. On August 28, the uprising started in Chiatura as well. For 3 days, the Chiaturians were able to restrain the punitive squads sent from Tbilisi to suppress the rebellion, however, on the 4th day, the Georgian Tseka suppressed the rebellion and brutally attacked the people participating in the demonstrations, shooting up to 2000 ChiaturiansDuring the Soviet period, Chiatura was a republican subordinate city and the center of the Chiatura City Council. In 1954, the first passenger cableway in the USSR was built in Chiatura, between the city center and the industrial district of Perevis. The number of passenger ropeways in the surroundings of Chiaturi is more than two dozen, which is one of the highest indicators in the world[1]. The drawing up and construction of Chiaturi passenger suspension cableway projects were carried out by local engineers. Giorgi Fantsulaia was an outstanding specialist in this matter, with the project of which ropeways were built both in Chiatura and in other cities of Russia. In 1964, Chiatura was connected to Sachkhere by a trolleybus line. This was the first intercity trolleybus line in the Georgian SSR.

The legend of the origin of the city's name is very interesting. It is said that when a passenger, probably a Rachveli who went hunting in the mountains of Zemo Imereti, saw the silvery river roaring from the top of the mountain in the valley, he asked in surprise: "Is this a worm or what?" The legend is quite popular and has become so popular among the city's population that they even consider it to be true. Chiaturi districts are connected to the central part of the city by cableways. The cableways here were opened in the Soviet period and are still working and are of great interest to tourists. It is believed that the first passenger aerial cableway in the Soviet Union was built in Chiatura in 1953. The ancient passenger-cableway called 25 is especially interesting, which is currently being better rehabilitated, although its authentic appearance will be fully preserved and it will be the only one. , which revives the Soviet period. Chiaturi House of Culture is a cultural institution located in the center of the Georgian city of Chiaturi. The building, built in the style of Soviet architecture, is an important object for local residents and visitors to the city. The cultural center hosts various events aimed at developing and maintaining the cultural life of the city. Here you can attend theater performances, concerts, exhibitions, film screenings and other creative events. The organization also provides rehearsal spaces and creative studios. The location is ideal for those interested in culture and art, as well as for organizers of cultural and social events. Residents and visitors of Chiaturi can not only enjoy a variety of programs here, but also become participants in various cultural initiatives and projects. Chiaturi House of Culture is open to the public during the week; Admission to most events is free or affordable, making cultural events accessible to a wide audience. A visit to the Chiaturi Culture Center will give you the opportunity to learn about the city's rich cultural life and enjoy the works of local and tourist artists

The main role in the economy of the municipality is played by industry, in particular, mining and enrichment of manganese ore. Manganese ore mining has a history of more than 130 years. Other useful minerals are also mined: marble and quartz sand. Quartz sand is used in various types of construction as concrete filler and plastering material. Brown marble, which is a precious paving material, is found in the village of Salieti. The Chiatura-Sachkhere-Gomi road of state importance passes through the territory of the municipality. The length of roads of local importance is 270 km

The establishment of the museum in 1962 was caused by the rich archaeological and ethnographic material found in the territory of Chiaturi district. Protected: archaeological, ethnographic, numismatic, photographic and written materials, works of painting and applied art. Since the 50s of the last century, a number of archaeological expeditions have been carried out in the Shout Basin. The expeditions were successful. Abundant material was found in the territory of the district, a large part of which was placed in the National Museum named after Janashia, as well as in the Museum of Local Lore in Chiaturi. Historical and cultural artefacts found in the territory of Chiaturi by the local population were added to it. Archaeological collection: The archeological collection has a special place in the Museum of Local Lore of Chiaturi. The collection includes various exhibits. This is both randomly obtained material and material obtained from archaeological expeditions. Artifacts include the Stone, Bronze and Iron periods. Numismatic collection: The fund of the museum is rich in numismatic collection, the collection includes Georgian, Arabic and Persian coins, Georgian white.
